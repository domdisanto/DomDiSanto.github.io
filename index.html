I am a PhD student in the Department of Biostatistics at Harvard University, co-advised by Junwei Lu and Tianxi Cai. My research focuses on statistical theory and methodology to inform different (biomedically-relevant) decision processes. This often requires drawing on (or extending) nonparametfic and high-dimensional statistical theory to building inferential tools for problems such as:

  • Identifying an optimal assortment of clinical-decision aids, accounting for provider preferences and clinical utility
  • Assessing the explanatory stability for different model classes in statistical learning problems
I am currently working with colleagues in the Immunology and Infectious Diseases department, using AI to analyze high-dimensional immunological data as well as inform data collection for peptide-based assays.

Previously, I studied Neuroscience and Biostatistics at the University of Pittsburgh. I have worked as an applied statistician for in an epidemiological research group studying traumatic brain injuries and later at the Center for Health Equity and Research Promotion at the Department of Veterans Affairs.
